    Frequently Asked Questions

  • +My BRIO 500/501 Webcam video flickers

    If you experience flickering lights in your webcam video, you can adjust the Anti-flicker setting in Tune. The frequency of your lights should be synchronized with your webcam to avoid having visible flickering on your video. This means you have basically instructed the camera you are using to take a picture every time the ON cycle happens.

    In order to achieve synchronization you can choose between two settings:

    • NTSC 60HZ
    • PAL 50HZ

    Depending on your location and environment this will vary, but Logi Tune will let you select either of the two options.

  • +My video is not as sharp and fluid as expected

    Your BRIO 500/501 streams with HDR in real-time. There is a tradeoff between frame rate and exposure.

  • +How does the new Show Mode feature work?

    Show mode is a feature that lets you showcase your desk and sketches of physical objects simply by tilting the angle of the camera. The built-in accelerometer will flip the video feed to ensure that the viewer will see the image correctly. Show Mode is not designed to scan documents or read very fine prints.

    If your image is not clear or showing up well, make sure there is enough light on the desk or the object you want to showcase.

    Virtual backgrounds or blurring filters will interfere with the feature. Make sure you disable these before you use Show Mode for a better sharing experience. We are working with our partners to provide an updated solution on this.

  • +Why are meeting invites not visible in Logi Tune?

    To get the meeting notification, Tune needs to be connected to your calendar.

    1. Make sure the meeting is visible in your connected calendar. If it's not visible, try to sync the connected calendar to the Microsoft Teams or Outlook calendar.
    2. Refresh your Agenda view by scrolling down in the dashboard and clicking Refresh.
    3. Try reconnecting your calendar. In Tune settings, go to Calendar connection > Disconnect. Now try reconnecting your calendar.

  • +Why is the Join Meeting shortcut not working?

    Tune allows you to join meetings using keyboard shortcuts like Alt+J (Windows) or Cmd +J (Mac).

    If your computer is configured to use a similar shortcut to perform a different function, the shortcut may not work. Please check on your operating system to verify no other apps are using the same shortcut.

  • +How do I set up multiple calendars in Logi Tune?

    You can sync your secondary calendar with the primary calendar you use with Tune.

    Follow the steps for your calendar:

    • Google Calendar
    • Microsoft Calendar

  • +Which video conferencing apps support One Click to Join?

    Tune currently supports Zoom, Google Meet and Microsoft Teams for One Click to Join meetings.

  • +My meeting notifications aren’t working

    If you aren't receiving meeting notifications, check the following:

    1. Make sure your computer is not in Do Not Disturb (DND) mode.
    2. Try reconnecting your calendar. In Tune settings, go to Calendar connection > Disconnect. Then try reconnecting your calendar.

  • +Which calendars does Logi Tune Agenda support?

    The Agenda feature works with Google Calendar and the Microsoft Outlook calendar.

  • +Webcam software update fails in Logi Tune

    If the update fails, try the following:

    1. Quit the app — right-click the headset icon and select Quit.
    2. Re-open the app and try the update again.
    3. If you continue to experience this issue, restart your computer and try again, or reach out to  Customer Support.

  • +My product is not detected by Logi Tune

    Logi Tune supports only the following products:

    Webcams:

    C920e, C920c, C925e, C930e, BRIO, BRIO 500, BRIO 501, BRIO 505 and more

    Headsets:

    Zone Wireless, Zone Wireless Plus, Zone Wired and more

    If your BRIO 500/501 Webcam is plugged into a USB-C port, and after several minutes the device is not recognized in the system or does not show up in your applications (ex. Zoom, MS Teams), try the following to check if the problem is is a connection issue rather than a problem with your hardware:

    • Check your setup — are all cables and connections plugged in tightly?
    • If a BRIO 500/501 Webcam is connected through a USB hub or extender, try plugging directly into the USB port — some external hubs or extenders may not have adequate power or specifications to support BRIO 500/501 Webcam.
    • Try a different computer — does the BRIO 500/501 Webcam work fine on another system?

    For Windows:
    You can use Device Manager in Windows to view and control the hardware attached to the computer. When a piece of hardware is not working, it may not be listed or may show a yellow exclamation point next to the identified hardware to indicate there is an issue.

    1. Open Device Manager by selecting the Windows Start icon, then typing “Device Manager” in the search bar to the right of the start icon.
    2. Open Imaging Devices and confirm if Logitech BRIO 500/501 Webcam is listed. If it's not listed:
      • Try a different USB port
      • Check if Windows Update is enabled:
        Windows Update is a Microsoft service that automatically downloads and installs updates over the internet to the operating system. You can access Windows Update by selecting the Windows start icon and typing in Windows Update in the search field. Windows Update settings are controlled by the Administrator of your system, so if you are working in a professional environment, you may need to contact your IT department.
    3. If Logitech BRIO 500/501 Webcam is listed but has a yellow exclamation point, then try the following:
      1. In Device Manager, right-click on BRIO 500/501 Webcam/UNKNOWN device with the yellow exclamation icon, and select Update Driver.
      2. In the Update dialog, select Update Driver.
      3. Select Let me pick from a list of available drivers on my computer.
      4. Check Show compatible hardware (if not checked), then select USB Video Device (not Logitech BRIO 500/501 Webcam), from the list.
      5. Click Next.
        NOTE: If a list of categories is shown instead, select Imaging Devices, then USB Video Device.
    4. If Logitech BRIO 500/501 Webcam is listed without a yellow exclamation point, try the following to make sure the latest updated driver is installed:
      1. In Device Manager, right-click on the BRIO 500/501 Webcam device, and select Update Driver.
      2. In the Update dialog, select Update Driver.
      3. Select Search automatically for updated driver software.
      4. Select the Logitech BRIO 500/501 Webcam driver that is listed.

    For macOS:

    1. Select the Apple icon at the top left of the screen.
    2. Select About this Mac in the drop-down menu, and then System Report in the pop-up box.
    3. In the System Report, if the camera is properly recognized, it will be listed under Camera as Logitech BRIO 500/501 Webcam.
    4. On older Mac versions, you may see Logitech Camera instead of BRIO 500/501 Webcam as the video device. In order to select BRIO 500/501 Webcam as your video device, select Logitech Camera.

    If you are using one of the above listed products and are still having an issue, please contact our support team or report the issue within the Tune app by going to Settings > Feedback.

  • +My BRIO 500/501 video looks out of focus

    If your camera output appears blurry or the focus will not resolve to a clear, non-blurry image, try to trigger the autofocus — hold your hand in front of the lens and then remove it. The focus on the camera is automatic and should adjust itself.

    If the camera does not self-adjust, try manually adjusting focus:

    1. Download and install the Logi Tune app.
    2. Once installed, turn autofocus off in the Logi Tune app and then adjust the focus manually by adjusting the slider in the app.

    In addition, the zoom function in BRIO 500/501 Webcam is digital. The image quality may lose its sharpness as you zoom in from 1X-5X, just as you experience on a smartphone.

    NOTE: Please make sure you have the latest BRIO 500/501 Webcam firmware from the Logi Tune app.

  • +My BRIO 500/501 Webcam video does not look right

    There are many reasons why video may not look the way you expect. Are the colors not right? Does the video break up? Is there noise?

    The most likely cause of poor video quality can be due to one or more of the following:

    • Video calling application — refer to the help content for your video conferencing application or contact the video conferencing application’s support for advice on how to achieve HD quality.
    • USB Bandwidth — too many active USB devices being used at the same time can affect the overall quality of your video stream. Disconnect or turn off any USB devices not in use.
    • CPU utilization — too many processes running on your CPU can affect the overall quality of your video stream. Terminate any applications or processes you may not be using.
    • Network bandwidth — not having enough network bandwidth can affect the overall quality of your stream.

      Try these tips to ensure the camera is working as it should:

    • Verify that the network or video conferencing service is not the cause of the video quality issues. You can test by using a video application such as the Windows 10 Camera Application and check the video quality locally.
    • Make sure that the video is streaming through your BRIO 500/501 camera and not another camera such as your laptop camera or another attached camera.
    • Confirm that you’re using the latest firmware for BRIO 500/501 Webcam. If you’re not sure, you can check if your webcam is up-to-date from Logi Tune.

  • +I’m not seeing 1080p resolution with BRIO 500/501 Webcam

    Your Logitech BRIO 500/501 Webcam supports Full HD (1080p) video at 30 frames per second. If you’re not seeing 1080p resolution, it may be that your video conferencing application supports up to 720p or there might even be a setting within the application to put the video into HD quality. Please check your application requirements and settings.

    In addition, check the following:

    • USB bandwidth — (ex. using many active USB devices at the same time)
    • CPU utilization — (ex. file transfers, streaming, checking email, antivirus scanning)
    • Network bandwidth — your internet connection download speed should be at least 3 Mbps

  • +My BRIO 500/501 video appears darker or lighter than expected

    Because BRIO 500/501 streams with HDR in real-time, you may experience video that is lighter or darker than expected in scenes with heavy contrast (ex. a lot of white or light colors and/or darker colors and tones). The camera may adjust the scene in a way to compensate for the contrast extremes which can cause the entire scene to become lighter or darker.

    One thing you can do is introduce more natural back and side lighting. This will trigger the HDR feature to make the appropriate adjustments. You can also try sitting closer to the camera — try 30 inches and adjust closer or further to see what works best.

  • +My BRIO 500/501 does not connect or is not recognized by my computer

    Make sure your BRIO 500/501 is connected directly into your computer's USB-C port or in an adapter with a USB-C port that supports both power and video. Some adapters only support power, in which case BRIO 500/501 is not recognized.

  • +BRIO 500/501 LED status indicator

    The LEDs on BRIO 500/501 indicate the status of your webcam:

    • Solid white: Outgoing video stream
    • Solid yellow: Outgoing audio stream (No video)
    • Breathing purple: Updating firmware

  • +Can I use a USB-C to USB-A adapter with my BRIO 500/501 webcam?

    You can connect your BRIO 500/501 through a docking station with a USB-C and adapters. Make sure the adapter you choose supports both power and video. Some adapters do not support video, in which case your camera will not appear.

  • +Can I change the cable for my BRIO 500/501?

    You can't change the cable on BRIO 500/501 — it's attached to the camera. You can use an adapter, however. Make sure the adapter you choose supports both power and video. Some adapters do not support video, in which case your camera will not appear.

  • +Does the mount suction pad leave any residue on my monitor?

    The mount for your webcam is designed with a micro suction pad to keep it secure on the monitor. The sticky pad helps keep the mount in place when you pan or tilt the webcam angle.

    The microsuction sticky pad is reusable and cleanable, and it does not leave any residue on your monitor. Make sure to clean it when you move it to a new place so it continues to stick well.

    To clean the micro-suction pad, use tap water and soap. Please avoid using any cleaning products as it may reduce the performance of the pad.

  • +Connect the BRIO 500/501

    Your BRIO 500/501 webcam comes with a USB-C connector. Plug it into an available port on your computer. If your webcam is connected through a USB hub or extender, make sure the USB-C port supports both power and video.
